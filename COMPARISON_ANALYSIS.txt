COMPREHENSIVE COMPARISON: Traditional vs Novel MAP Pruning
================================================================================

EXPERIMENT DETAILS:
- Dataset: CIFAR-10
- Model: ResNet-20 (272,474 parameters)
- Training: 30 epochs initial, 10 epochs fine-tuning
- Pruning Ratios Tested: 30%, 50%, 70%, 90%
- Device: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3050 Laptop GPU
- Total Experiment Time: 24.80 minutes

- Prune weights with smallest absolute values
- Fine-tune for 10 epochs after pruning

================================================================================
METHOD 2: NOVEL MAP (Multi-Criteria Attention-based Pruning)
================================================================================

Strategy:
- Multi-head attention combining 4 criteria:
  1. Magnitude Attention: |w| / max(|w|)
  2. Gradient Attention: |∂ℓ/∂w| / max(|∂ℓ/∂w|)
  3. Entropy Attention: Information-theoretic diversity
  4. Hessian Attention: Second-order curvature (Fisher information)
- Adaptive fusion weight scheduling across training phases
- Progressive pruning during training (at epochs 10 and 20)

Original Model:
- Test Accuracy: 88.29%
- Parameters: 272,474

Results After Training with Progressive Pruning:

================================================================================
VERDICT: TRADITIONAL MAGNITUDE PRUNING WINS
================================================================================

Reasons for Traditional Method's Superior Performance:

1. ✓ POST-PRUNING FINE-TUNING IS CRITICAL
   - Traditional method: Prune once, then fine-tune intensively
   - Novel MAP: Progressive pruning during training
   - Fine-tuning allows network to recover from pruning shock

2. ✓ SIMPLICITY WINS FOR THIS ARCHITECTURE
   - Magnitude-only criteria sufficient for ResNet-20
   - Multi-criteria attention adds complexity without benefit
   - Over-engineering for a relatively simple task

3. ⚠ NOVEL MAP IMPLEMENTATION ISSUES IDENTIFIED:
   - All models stuck in "early" phase → fusion weights not adapting
   - Progressive pruning may disrupt learning trajectory
   - Entropy attention may be miscalculating importance

4. ✓ TRADITIONAL METHOD MORE ROBUST
   - Consistent 88-89% accuracy across all sparsity levels
   - Minimal accuracy drop even at 90% sparsity
   - Proven, battle-tested approach

================================================================================
WHY NOVEL MAP UNDERPERFORMED (Root Cause Analysis)
================================================================================

1. TRAINING STRATEGY MISMATCH:
   - Novel MAP: Prunes during training → disrupts learned features
   - Traditional: Prunes after training → preserves learned features
   
2. FUSION WEIGHT SCHEDULING NOT WORKING:
   - All models show "early" phase throughout training
   - Adaptive scheduling failed to trigger phase transitions
   - Gradient/Entropy/Hessian weights may be poorly calibrated

3. PROGRESSIVE PRUNING HARM:
   - Pruning at epochs 10 and 20 causes re-learning
   - Network must adapt to changing structure mid-training
   - Traditional one-shot + fine-tune is more stable

4. ATTENTION MECHANISM ISSUES:
   - Gradient attention may emphasize wrong weights
   - Entropy calculation may not capture true importance
   - Hessian approximation via Fisher may be inaccurate

================================================================================
RECOMMENDATIONS FOR IMPROVING NOVEL MAP
================================================================================

To make Novel MAP competitive, consider these fixes:

1. CHANGE TRAINING STRATEGY:
   ✓ Apply pruning AFTER initial training (like traditional)
   ✓ Use multi-criteria attention for mask selection only
   ✓ Then fine-tune with frozen masks

2. FIX PHASE SCHEDULING:
   ✓ Debug why phase remains "early" throughout
   ✓ Verify epoch_fraction calculation
   ✓ Add logging for fusion weight transitions

3. CALIBRATE ATTENTION HEADS:
   ✓ Verify gradient attention accumulation
   ✓ Check entropy calculation correctness
   ✓ Validate Hessian approximation

4. HYBRID APPROACH:
   ✓ Use Novel MAP attention for initial mask selection
   ✓ Apply traditional fine-tuning strategy afterward
   ✓ Best of both worlds: smart pruning + robust recovery

5. ABLATION STUDIES:
   ✓ Test each attention head individually
   ✓ Compare magnitude-only vs multi-criteria
   ✓ Evaluate progressive vs one-shot pruning

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Despite the theoretical advantages of multi-criteria attention-based pruning,
the TRADITIONAL MAGNITUDE-BASED PRUNING method significantly outperformed the
Novel MAP approach in this experiment.

Key Takeaway: Sometimes simpler is better. The combination of magnitude-based
pruning + intensive fine-tuning is highly effective for ResNet-20 on CIFAR-10.

However, this doesn't invalidate multi-criteria pruning research. The Novel MAP
implementation has identifiable issues that, if fixed, could potentially show
improvements on larger, more complex models where simple magnitude may be
insufficient.

Final Recommendation: For ResNet-20 CIFAR-10 pruning, stick with traditional
magnitude pruning. The results speak for themselves: 88-89% accuracy maintained
across all sparsity levels, including 90% pruning.
